# CBT Techniques and Interventions

## Cognitive Restructuring
Identify and challenge negative automatic thoughts.
Steps:
1. Identify the situation and automatic thought
2. Examine the evidence for and against the thought
3. Generate alternative, balanced thoughts
4. Re-rate emotional intensity

## Behavioral Activation
Increase engagement in positive activities to improve mood.
Steps:
1. Monitor current activities and mood
2. Identify values and meaningful activities
3. Schedule pleasant and mastery activities
4. Start small and build gradually
5. Track mood changes

## Grounding Techniques (5-4-3-2-1)
Reduce anxiety by focusing on present moment.
Steps:
1. Name 5 things you can see
2. Name 4 things you can touch
3. Name 3 things you can hear
4. Name 2 things you can smell
5. Name 1 thing you can taste

## Progressive Muscle Relaxation
Reduce physical tension through systematic muscle relaxation.
Steps:
1. Tense muscle group for 5 seconds
2. Release and notice the difference
3. Move through all major muscle groups
4. Practice daily for best results

## Thought Records
Structured way to examine and challenge thoughts.
Columns:
- Situation
- Automatic Thought
- Emotion (0-100)
- Evidence For
- Evidence Against
- Alternative Thought
- Re-rate Emotion

## Exposure Therapy
Gradually face feared situations to reduce anxiety.
Steps:
1. Create fear hierarchy (0-100)
2. Start with low-anxiety situations
3. Stay in situation until anxiety decreases
4. Practice regularly
5. Progress up the hierarchy

## Problem-Solving Therapy
Structured approach to solving life problems.
Steps:
1. Define the problem clearly
2. Brainstorm solutions (no judgment)
3. Evaluate pros and cons
4. Choose and implement solution
5. Review outcome

## Mindfulness Meditation
Cultivate present-moment awareness.
Practice:
- Focus on breath
- Notice thoughts without judgment
- Return attention to breath
- Start with 5 minutes daily

## Activity Scheduling
Plan activities to improve mood and functioning.
Include:
- Pleasant activities (enjoyment)
- Mastery activities (achievement)
- Social activities (connection)
- Self-care activities (health)

## Socratic Questioning
Guide discovery through questions.
Examples:
- What's the evidence?
- What would you tell a friend?
- What's the worst that could happen?
- How likely is that really?
- What's a more balanced view?
