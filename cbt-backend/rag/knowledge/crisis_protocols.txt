# Crisis Protocols and Safety Planning

## Warning Signs of Crisis
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
- Feeling hopeless or trapped
- Unbearable emotional pain
- Talking about being a burden
- Withdrawing from others
- Giving away possessions
- Saying goodbye
- Seeking access to lethal means

## Immediate Safety Steps
If someone expresses suicidal thoughts:
1. Take it seriously - never dismiss or minimize
2. Ask directly: "Are you thinking about suicide?"
3. Listen without judgment
4. Don't leave them alone if in immediate danger
5. Remove access to lethal means
6. Get professional help immediately

## Safety Planning
Create a written safety plan including:
1. Warning signs that crisis is developing
2. Internal coping strategies (without others)
3. People and social settings that provide distraction
4. People to ask for help
5. Professionals or agencies to contact
6. Ways to make environment safe

## Grounding in Crisis
When overwhelmed:
- 5-4-3-2-1 sensory grounding
- Cold water on face
- Hold ice cube
- Deep breathing (4-7-8 technique)
- Call someone safe
- Go to safe place

## Professional Help
When to seek immediate professional help:
- Active suicidal thoughts with plan
- Severe self-harm urges
- Psychotic symptoms
- Severe panic attacks
- Unable to function in daily life
- Substance abuse crisis

## Therapist Limitations
AI and chatbots cannot:
- Provide emergency intervention
- Replace human therapists
- Prescribe medication
- Diagnose mental health conditions
- Provide ongoing therapy
- Handle acute crisis situations

Always refer to human professionals for:
- Suicidal ideation
- Severe mental health symptoms
- Medication management
- Trauma processing
- Complex mental health conditions
